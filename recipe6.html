Penne Arrabbiata

⏱ Prep: 5 min 🔥 Cook: 20 min 🍳 Serves: 4 ● Easy
← Back to Recipes

Ingredients

  • 400g penne rigate
  • 400g San Marzano crushed tomatoes
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 2 tsp dried red chili flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Small bunch fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped
  • Coarse salt (for pasta water)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Pecorino Romano, grated (optional)

How to Make It

Arrabbiata means "angry" in Italian — and this sauce has the heat to prove it.

01

Infuse the Oil

Heat extra virgin olive oil in a wide, deep pan over medium-low heat. Add the thinly sliced garlic and chili flakes. Cook gently for 2–3 minutes, stirring frequently, until the garlic is fragrant and just starting to turn golden. Do not let it burn — bitter garlic ruins the dish.

02

Build the Sauce

Add the San Marzano tomatoes to the pan, crushing any large pieces with the back of a spoon. Season with a pinch of salt. Increase heat to medium and simmer for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate on top — a sign the sauce is ready.

03

Cook the Pasta

While the sauce simmers, bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a rolling boil. Cook penne rigate until al dente — about 1 minute less than the packet suggests. Reserve ¼ cup of the pasta cooking water before draining.

04

Marry Pasta and Sauce

Add the drained penne directly to the arrabbiata sauce. Toss well over medium heat for 1–2 minutes so the pasta absorbs the sauce. Add a splash of pasta water if needed to loosen and create a light glaze that clings to every tube.

05

Garnish and Serve

Remove from heat. Stir in a generous handful of freshly chopped flat-leaf parsley. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve immediately in warm bowls with a drizzle of extra olive oil and grated Pecorino Romano on the side for those who want it.
