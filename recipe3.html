Tiramisu

⏱ Prep: 30 min ❄ Chill: 4+ hours 🍳 Serves: 8 ● Easy
← Back to Recipes

Ingredients

  • 300g Savoiardi ladyfinger biscuits
  • 500g mascarpone cheese
  • 4 large eggs, separated
  • 100g caster sugar
  • 300ml strong espresso, cooled
  • 2 tbsp Marsala wine (optional)
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting

How to Make It

No baking required — just patience while it chills in the fridge.

01

Brew the Espresso

Make 300ml of strong espresso and let it cool completely to room temperature. Stir in the Marsala wine if using. Pour into a shallow bowl for dipping — you'll need quick access to this later.

02

Beat the Yolks

Using a hand or stand mixer, beat the egg yolks and sugar together for 4–5 minutes until the mixture is pale, thick, and ribbon-like. Fold in the mascarpone cheese gently until smooth and no lumps remain. Set aside.

03

Whip the Egg Whites

In a clean, grease-free bowl, whisk the egg whites to stiff peaks — they should hold their shape when the beaters are lifted. Gently fold the egg whites into the mascarpone mixture in three additions to keep it light and airy.

04

Dip the Ladyfingers

Working quickly, dip each ladyfinger into the cooled espresso for 1–2 seconds per side. They should be moist but not soggy. Arrange a single layer in the base of a rectangular dish (approximately 20x30cm).

05

Layer the Cream

Spread half of the mascarpone cream evenly over the ladyfinger layer. Repeat with a second layer of soaked ladyfingers, followed by the remaining cream. Smooth the top with a spatula.

06

Chill and Serve

Dust the top generously with unsweetened cocoa powder using a fine sieve. Cover and refrigerate for a minimum of 4 hours — overnight is even better. Serve chilled, dusting with a little extra cocoa just before bringing to the table.
