Risotto ai Funghi

⏱ Prep: 10 min 🔥 Cook: 30 min 🍳 Serves: 4 ● Hard
← Back to Recipes

Ingredients

  • 300g Arborio rice
  • 30g dried porcini mushrooms
  • 200g fresh cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 150ml dry white wine
  • 1L vegetable broth, kept hot
  • 60g Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated
  • 40g cold unsalted butter
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • Salt and black pepper

How to Make It

Patience is key — risotto demands constant attention and cannot be rushed.

01

Rehydrate the Porcini

Soak dried porcini mushrooms in 250ml of warm water for 20 minutes. Once soft, remove and roughly chop. Strain the soaking liquid through a fine sieve or coffee filter to remove grit — this liquid is liquid gold and goes into the risotto.

02

Sauté the Aromatics and Mushrooms

Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a wide, heavy-based pan over medium heat. Cook onion and garlic for 5 minutes until soft and translucent. Add fresh cremini mushrooms and cook for 5–6 minutes until golden and any moisture has evaporated. Add the rehydrated porcini and stir.

03

Toast the Rice

Add the Arborio rice to the pan and stir for 2 minutes over medium-high heat until the grains are well coated in oil and look slightly translucent at the edges. Toasting develops nutty flavour and helps the rice hold its texture.

04

Add Wine and Porcini Liquid

Pour in the white wine and stir until fully absorbed. Then add the strained porcini soaking liquid and stir until absorbed. This builds deep, earthy mushroom flavour in the base of the risotto.

05

Add Broth Gradually

Add the hot vegetable broth one ladle at a time, stirring constantly and waiting for each addition to be absorbed before adding the next. This process takes 18–20 minutes. The rice should be creamy but still have a slight bite (al dente) in the centre.

06

Mantecatura — The Finishing Step

Remove from heat. Add the cold butter and grated Parmigiano-Reggiano. Stir vigorously for 1–2 minutes to emulsify — this is what gives risotto its signature glossy, creamy consistency. Season with salt and pepper, garnish with fresh parsley, and serve immediately.
