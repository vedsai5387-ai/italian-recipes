Margherita Pizza

⏱ Prep: 30 min 🔥 Cook: 15 min 🍳 Serves: 2 ● Medium
← Back to Recipes

Ingredients

  • 300g 00 flour (or strong bread flour)
  • 7g dry active yeast
  • 200ml warm water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 200g San Marzano canned tomatoes
  • 200g fresh mozzarella, torn
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil (to finish)
  • Salt and black pepper

How to Make It

A proper Neapolitan Margherita — blistered crust, minimal toppings, maximum flavour.

01

Make the Dough

Dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let sit 5 minutes until foamy. Mix flour and salt, then add yeast mixture and olive oil. Knead for 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover and rest for 1 hour until doubled in size.

02

Preheat Your Oven

Set your oven to its maximum temperature — ideally 250–280°C (480–530°F). Place a baking stone, steel, or a heavy baking tray inside to preheat for at least 30 minutes. A super-hot surface is the secret to a blistered crust.

03

Prepare the Tomato Sauce

Crush San Marzano tomatoes by hand into a bowl. Season with a pinch of salt, a little olive oil, and optionally a pinch of dried oregano. Do not cook the sauce — it goes on raw and cooks on the pizza.

04

Shape the Pizza

Punch down the dough and stretch it by hand on a floured surface into a thin 30cm round. Work from the centre outward, leaving a slightly thicker border for the crust. Transfer to a floured pizza peel or parchment paper.

05

Top and Bake

Spread a thin, even layer of tomato sauce, leaving the border bare. Scatter torn mozzarella pieces across the top. Slide onto the hot stone or tray and bake for 10–13 minutes until the crust is charred and blistered and the cheese is bubbling.

06

Finish and Serve

Remove from the oven and immediately scatter fresh basil leaves over the pizza. Drizzle generously with extra virgin olive oil. Slice and serve at once while the crust is still crisp.
