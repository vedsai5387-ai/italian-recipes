Lasagna al Forno

⏱ Prep: 45 min 🔥 Cook: 45 min 🍳 Serves: 8 ● Hard
← Back to Recipes

Ingredients

  • 500g dried lasagna sheets
  • 500g ground beef (minced)
  • 400g crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • 150ml dry red wine
  • 1 carrot, finely diced
  • 1 celery stalk, finely diced
  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 60g unsalted butter (for béchamel)
  • 60g plain flour
  • 700ml whole milk, warm
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • 150g Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated
  • Extra virgin olive oil, salt, pepper

How to Make It

A labour of love — but every layer is worth it. Best made the day before serving.

01

Make the Soffritto

Heat olive oil in a large heavy pan over medium heat. Add the finely diced onion, carrot, and celery. Cook for 8–10 minutes, stirring often, until softened, sweet, and lightly golden. This aromatic base is the soul of any good Bolognese.

02

Build the Bolognese

Increase heat to medium-high. Add ground beef and cook, breaking it apart, until well browned all over. Pour in red wine and let it evaporate fully — about 3 minutes. Add crushed tomatoes, season with salt and pepper, and simmer on low heat for 30 minutes until rich and thick.

03

Make the Béchamel

Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in flour and cook for 2 minutes to form a roux. Gradually add warm milk, whisking constantly to prevent lumps. Cook for 8–10 minutes until thick enough to coat the back of a spoon. Season with salt and a pinch of nutmeg. Remove from heat.

04

Preheat and Prep

Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Lightly grease a 30x20cm baking dish. If using dried lasagna sheets, pre-cook them in boiling salted water for 4–5 minutes until pliable but not fully cooked. Drain and lay flat on a clean cloth.

05

Layer the Lasagna

Spread a thin layer of béchamel on the base of the dish. Add a layer of pasta sheets, followed by Bolognese, then béchamel, then Parmigiano. Repeat this layering 4–5 times, finishing with a final layer of béchamel and a generous topping of Parmigiano.

06

Bake

Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for a further 15 minutes until the top is golden and bubbling with a slightly charred edge. This uncovered time creates the prized crispy cheese crust.

07

Rest and Serve

Allow the lasagna to rest for 15 minutes before cutting — this is essential to let it set so it holds its shape when served. Cut into generous squares and serve with a simple green salad.
