Spaghetti Carbonara

⏱ Prep: 15 min 🔥 Cook: 20 min 🍳 Serves: 4 ● Medium
← Back to Recipes

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g guanciale or pancetta, diced
  • 4 large eggs (room temperature)
  • 80g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
  • 40g Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper (generous)
  • Coarse salt (for pasta water)

How to Make It

Follow these steps closely — carbonara is all about technique and timing.

01

Boil the Pasta

Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente according to package instructions — usually 8–10 minutes. Reserve at least 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining.

02

Render the Guanciale

Place guanciale in a cold, large pan and turn the heat to medium. Cook for 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the fat renders out and the meat turns golden and crispy. Remove from heat and set aside — keep the rendered fat in the pan.

03

Make the Egg Mixture

In a bowl, whisk together the 4 eggs, Pecorino Romano, and Parmigiano-Reggiano. Add a generous amount of freshly cracked black pepper — this is a key flavour in carbonara. Set aside.

04

Combine Off the Heat

Add the hot drained spaghetti directly to the guanciale pan (off the heat — this is crucial to avoid scrambled eggs). Toss well to coat the pasta in the rendered fat.

05

Add the Egg Sauce

Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta. Toss quickly and continuously, adding reserved pasta water a splash at a time (start with 3–4 tablespoons). The residual heat will cook the eggs into a glossy, creamy sauce without curdling.

06

Serve Immediately

Plate the carbonara at once. Finish with extra Pecorino Romano, more cracked black pepper, and any remaining crispy guanciale on top. Serve immediately — carbonara waits for no one.
